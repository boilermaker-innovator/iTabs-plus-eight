How big is the area?

ℹ️ How to measure
📐
Measuring your area
Multiply length × width in metres. A standard double driveway is roughly 5m wide × 10m long = 50m². For odd shapes, break it into rectangles and add them up.
📱
Use Google Maps
Open Google Maps on desktop, right-click your property and select "Measure distance." Click around the area to get a rough measurement — it's surprisingly accurate.
📏 Common sizes
Single driveway: 30–40m² · Double driveway: 50–80m² · Patio: 15–30m² · Shed slab (6×9): 54m² · Path (1m wide, 15m long): 15m²

How thick?
▾
ℹ️ Which thickness do I need?
🏠
100mm — Standard residential
Fine for patios, paths, shed slabs, and light vehicle driveways. This is what most Perth homes use.
🚗
125mm — Heavy residential
Good for driveways with regular vehicle use, caravan parking, or if you want extra durability. Worth the small uplift.
🚛
150mm+ — Heavy duty
Required for commercial use, heavy vehicles, or council crossovers. Your concreter will advise if you need this.
⚠️ Don't skimp on thickness
A 100mm driveway that cracks from heavy use costs more to fix than getting 125mm upfront. If in doubt, go thicker — the material cost difference is small compared to the labour.

Any extras?
▾
ℹ️ What might I need?
🏗️ Old concrete removal
If you're replacing an existing driveway or slab, it needs to be demolished and disposed of. This adds $60–$90/m² depending on thickness and access. Some concreters include this in their quote, others don't — always ask.
🌊
Drainage
Perth's sandy soil drains well, but driveways and patios still need proper fall (slope) to avoid puddles. If your site is flat or slopes toward the house, you may need drainage channels — budget around $80–$150 per linear metre.
📋
Council permits
Crossovers almost always need council approval. Some councils also require permits for large slabs near boundaries. Check with your local council before starting — your concreter should know the rules for your area.

The Simple Idea

I'm Jon, a boilermaker from Perth. I built something simple that solves a universal problem: complex information creates friction everywhere.

iTabs is just a blue "i" button you add next to confusing terms. Click it, get a clean popup with the explanation. That's it. No apps to download, no accounts to create, no subscriptions.

The Insight: I was trying to build custom solutions for specific customers. Then I realized - why not just build a free generator that creates the code, and let people use it however they want?

Why This Approach Makes Sense

I'm a "Big Picture" Guy, Not a Details Guy

I realized something about myself: I'm great at seeing the vision and building prototypes, but I'm not the guy who wants to run infrastructure, manage user accounts, or do custom development for individual clients.

This generator approach plays to my strengths:

  • Build the tool once, everyone benefits
  • No ongoing support burden (users own their code)
  • No infrastructure to maintain (users host their own)
  • Can focus on making the generator better, not servicing accounts

Want People to Copy? That's Actually Good

Traditional business thinking says: "Protect your IP! Stop competitors!" But for iTabs, the opposite makes sense.

The more people copy iTabs, the better:

  • More websites have clearer information (the actual goal)
  • iTabs becomes a standard, like QR codes or social share buttons
  • Network effects: More familiarity = More adoption
  • Can't compete on scarcity, so compete on being the best generator

Think about it: QR codes are everywhere because anyone can generate them freely. If QR codes required accounts, subscriptions, and proprietary software, they'd never have taken off.

iTabs should be the same - ubiquitous because it's free and open.
